Inventory Tracking Guide

Manage stock levels, track supplies, and prevent stockouts

Quick Navigation
Overview Update Stock Low Stock Alerts Adjustments
Important

Accurate inventory tracking is critical for avoiding stockouts and overstock situations. Update inventory regularly to maintain accurate business records.

Understanding Your Inventory

Inventory Dashboard:

The Inventory Dashboard shows:

  • Total Stock Value: Total value of all inventory
  • Low Stock Items: Products below reorder level
  • Out of Stock: Items with zero quantity
  • Recent Movements: Latest inventory changes
  • Stock Turn Rate: How quickly items sell

Key Inventory Metrics:

Current Stock

The quantity you have on hand right now

Reorder Level

Minimum quantity before triggering alert

Reserved Stock

Items allocated to pending orders

Available Stock

Stock available for new orders (Current - Reserved)

Updating Stock Levels

Manual Stock Update:

1
Go to Inventory

Click Inventory in the main menu.

2
Find Product

Search or browse to find the product you want to update.

3
Click Edit Stock

Click the edit icon or "Update Stock" button.

4
Enter New Quantity

Enter the new stock quantity or adjust by amount.

5
Add Note (Optional)

Add reason for adjustment (e.g., "New batch received", "Damaged items removed")

6
Save

Click "Save" to update inventory.

Pro Tip

Always add a note when adjusting stock so you have a record of what happened and when.

Low Stock Alerts

The system automatically alerts you when items run low on stock.

Setting Reorder Levels:

1
Open Product

Go to Products and click on the product.

2
Edit Reorder Level

Find the "Reorder Level" field and enter the minimum quantity.

3
Save

Click Save. You'll now receive alerts when stock falls below this level.

Understanding Alerts:

Alert Type Condition Action
LOW STOCK Stock below reorder level Place reorder immediately
OUT OF STOCK Stock at zero Emergency reorder needed
OVERSTOCK Stock significantly above normal Consider promotions or adjustments

Alert Notifications:

You receive alerts through:

  • Dashboard: See alerts on your home screen
  • Email: Daily low stock report
  • Bell Icon: Real-time notifications

Stock Adjustments

Adjust inventory for various reasons like damage, theft, or discrepancies.

Common Adjustment Reasons:

Reason Action Example
Damaged Items Reduce stock Bread got moldy, remove 5 units
Shrinkage/Loss Reduce stock Missing inventory, adjust -3
New Batch Received Add to stock New delivery, add +50 units
Inventory Correction Manual adjustment Recount shows 25 not 30, adjust -5
Transfer In/Out Adjust by location Move between locations

Making Adjustments:

1

Go to Inventory → Adjustments

2

Click "New Adjustment"

3

Select product and adjustment type

4

Enter quantity and reason

5

Click "Save" to record adjustment

Physical Stock Count

Regularly count physical inventory and compare with system records.

Conducting a Count:

Stock Count Process
  1. Go to Inventory → Stock Count
  2. Click "Start New Count"
  3. Select products to count (all or specific)
  4. Print count sheet or use mobile app
  5. Count items physically
  6. Enter actual quantities in system
  7. System shows discrepancies
  8. Review and approve adjustments

Frequency Recommendations:

Cyclic Count

Count portion of inventory weekly or bi-weekly

Advantages: Easier to manage, less disruption

Full Count

Count all inventory monthly or quarterly

Advantages: Complete picture, comprehensive

Stock History & Reports

Viewing Stock Movements:

1
Go to Inventory

Navigate to Inventory section

2
Click on Product

Select the product to view history

3
View History Tab

See complete history of all changes, including:

  • Date and time of change
  • User who made change
  • Type of change (sale, return, adjustment)
  • Quantity changed
  • Notes/reason

Best Practices

DO's
  • ✓ Update inventory daily
  • ✓ Set appropriate reorder levels
  • ✓ Record all adjustments with notes
  • ✓ Perform regular stock counts
  • ✓ Review slow-moving items
  • ✓ Track expiration dates
DON'Ts
  • ✗ Forget to update inventory
  • ✗ Ignore low stock alerts
  • ✗ Make adjustments without notes
  • ✗ Skip physical counts
  • ✗ Keep obsolete items
  • ✗ Store expired products
